The Lithium Glossary

Updated: July 2026

The vocabulary of the lithium industry, in plain English. Terms are alphabetical; prices and contracts are covered at the end.

Resources and geology

Brine
Salty groundwater containing dissolved lithium, pumped from beneath salt flats. The dominant resource type in the Lithium Triangle.
Hard rock
Lithium hosted in solid minerals (mainly spodumene), mined conventionally and concentrated before conversion. Dominant in Australia.
Lepidolite
A lithium-bearing mica, generally lower grade and costlier to process than spodumene. Significant in China's domestic supply.
Lithium Triangle
The high-altitude region where Argentina, Bolivia and Chile meet, holding more than half of the world's known lithium resources in brine.
Puna
The Andean high plateau of northwest Argentina where the country's salares — and most of its lithium projects — sit, typically above 3,500 metres.
Reserves vs resources
Resources are estimated lithium in the ground; reserves are the portion proven economically extractable today. The gap between the two can be enormous.
Salar
A salt flat. The surface expression of a closed basin whose brines may contain commercial lithium concentrations.
Spodumene
The main hard-rock lithium mineral. Traded as ~6% Li₂O concentrate ("SC6"), the benchmark feedstock for converters.

Production and processing

Battery grade
Lithium chemicals pure enough (≥99.5% for carbonate) for cathode production. Sells at a premium over technical grade.
Commissioning
The phase between construction and commercial operation, when a plant is tested and tuned. Projects report progress as a percentage.
Conversion
Processing concentrate or brine into lithium chemicals. "Converters" are the mostly Chinese plants that perform it under contract or on their own account.
DLE (Direct Lithium Extraction)
Technologies that pull lithium from brine chemically instead of by evaporation — faster, higher-recovery, and central to new projects in all three Triangle countries.
Evaporation ponds
The vast coloured pools where brine is concentrated by sun and wind over 12–24 months before processing. The traditional route in Chile and Argentina.
LCE (Lithium Carbonate Equivalent)
The industry's common unit for comparing volumes across different lithium chemicals. Capacities and forecasts are usually quoted in tonnes of LCE.
Lithium carbonate (Li₂CO₃)
The most-traded lithium chemical, used for LFP cathodes among others. The China spot price is the market's reference.
Lithium hydroxide (LiOH)
The chemical preferred for high-nickel cathodes. Trades separately from carbonate, sometimes at a premium, sometimes at a discount.
Lithium metal
Pure elemental lithium. Production statistics quoted "in lithium content" or tonnes of metal are ~5.3× smaller than the same volume expressed as LCE.
Nameplate capacity
A plant's design output at full utilisation. Real production typically takes years of ramp-up to approach it.
Ramp-up
The period — often 2–3 years for brine — between first production and steady output at or near nameplate capacity.
Recovery
The share of lithium in the resource that ends up in the final product. Evaporation ponds recover roughly half; DLE developers promise much more.

Market and contracts

BESS
Battery energy storage systems — grid-scale batteries. The fastest-growing source of lithium demand alongside electric vehicles.
CIF / FOB
Price bases: CIF includes freight to the destination port (e.g. "CIF China, Japan, Korea"); FOB is the price loaded at the origin port.
Contract vs spot
Most volume moves under longer-term contracts; the spot market for immediate delivery is where price discovery — and volatility — happens.
GFEX
The Guangzhou Futures Exchange, home of the lithium carbonate futures contract that increasingly drives global price sentiment.
Offtake agreement
A commitment to buy a project's future production, often signed years before start-up. Key to financing — and a map of who has locked up supply.
Swing supply
Higher-cost capacity that shuts when prices fall and restarts when they recover — in practice, much of Australia's and China's marginal production.
Tolling
Paying a converter a fee to process your concentrate into chemicals while you keep ownership of the material.

Policy and players

CBC
Contemporary Brunp CATL — the Chinese consortium building Bolivia's first large direct-extraction plants at Uyuni.
RIGI
Argentina's large-investment incentive regime, granting qualifying projects long-term tax, customs and currency-access stability. Most new lithium capacity is structured under it.
SQM–Codelco venture
The state-majority partnership that controls lithium production at Chile's Salar de Atacama through 2060.
YLB
Yacimientos de Litio Bolivianos, Bolivia's state lithium company, owner of majority stakes in every Uyuni project.
