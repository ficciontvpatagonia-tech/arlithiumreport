Argentina's Lithium Output Jumped 66% in 2025 — and the 2026 Pipeline Is Even Bigger

Production climbed from 13,800 to 23,000 tonnes of lithium metal, the fastest growth of any producer. Installed capacity now heads from 159,000 toward 400,000 tonnes of LCE by 2030.

By J. Martineau · AR Lithium Report

Argentina posted the strongest lithium production growth in the world in 2025: output rose 66%, from 13,800 to 23,000 tonnes of lithium metal, as new capacity across Salta, Jujuy and Catamarca ramped up in unison.

The surge consolidates a shift that the industry has watched for years. While Chile remains the region's largest producer, Argentina is the one adding tonnes fastest — the result of an open investment regime and a project pipeline that keeps maturing quarter after quarter.

What comes online in 2026

This year adds a new wave: Eramet's Centenario-Ratones expansion, POSCO's Sal de Oro and Ganfeng's Mariana are all scheduled to contribute first or additional volumes, alongside expansions at existing operations in the three lithium provinces.

Installed capacity currently stands at roughly 159,000 tonnes of lithium carbonate equivalent (LCE) per year. Officials and companies project that figure to surpass 400,000 tonnes by 2030 if the current pipeline holds — a number that would place Argentina firmly among the top producers globally.

The RIGI effect

Behind the acceleration sits RIGI, the large-investment incentive regime that grants tax and currency-access stability to qualifying projects. Most of the new lithium capacity announced since 2024 has been structured under it, and executives consistently cite it as the reason Argentina wins capital that once defaulted to Chile or Australia.

Growth of this speed is not without questions — water use in the Puna, infrastructure bottlenecks and community relations remain open fronts. But the direction is unambiguous: the country that produced under 14,000 tonnes two years ago is building toward almost thirty times that in installed capacity.
